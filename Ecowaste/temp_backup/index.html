Waste Management Basics

Types of Dustbins and What Goes Inside

Correct bin selection is the first step in smart waste management and cleaner cities.

Blue Bin

Paper, cardboard, magazines, and clean cartons.

Green Bin

Food scraps, fruit peels, leaves, and garden waste.

Yellow Bin

Plastic bottles, cans, wrappers, and dry mixed recyclables.

Red Bin

Hazardous waste: batteries, sanitary waste, medical disposable.

Waste Management Tips

  • Rinse food containers before recycling.
  • Do not mix wet and dry waste together.
  • Flatten cartons to save collection space.
  • Store e-waste separately for pickup.

Smart Bin Step 2 Flow

  • Step 1 scans the waste item and predicts material.
  • Step 2 scans a smart-bin image (not video) before credits unlock.
  • Credits are awarded only when Step 1 and Step 2 outputs match.
  • Location validation is required to prevent fake or repeated claims.

The Crisis

Why Your Actions Matter

Over 220 million tonnes of plastic waste are generated globally each year, yet only 9% gets recycled. The rest ends up in our oceans, landfills, and ecosystems.

Ocean Crisis

11 million metric tons of plastic enter our oceans annually, killing 300,000 marine mammals yearly through ingestion and entanglement.

171 trillion plastic particles in ocean

Climate Impact

Plastic production generates emissions equal to 615 coal-fired power stations. Recycling plastic reduces energy use by 66% compared to virgin production.

2.5 tons CO₂ saved per ton recycled

Resource Conservation

Recycling 1 ton of plastic saves 7.4 cubic yards of landfill space and 3.8 barrels of oil. It takes 75% less energy than making new plastic.

Powers a home for 7 months
